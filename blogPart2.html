How Love Taught Me About
The Lessons I Never Expected
Authored by: Luis Michael L. Suing | January 19, 2026

Love is what I yearned for

I never thought that love made me yearn on about it, just to feel that effortless bare minimum, moments that is being shared, seeking comfort, and etc. It made me believed that by having this kind of love, makes me the one to be "the winner takes it all". I trusted the feeling completely, letting myself fall without hesitation. Love felt like a promise, and I held onto it with certainty, unaware that it would soon ask more of me than I was prepared to give.

  • Yearning for genuine love and emotional connection
  • Desire for shared moments, comfort, and reassurance
  • Belief that love would bring fulfillment and certainty
  • Trusting love fully without hesitation or doubt
  • Holding onto the idea that love meant winning and belonging

Where Loving Meant Losing

Throughout the relationship that I have been, I had a realization that whenever I love someone, is the cost of losing myself for them just to be dependent, and vulnerable to them. As part of myself to love somebody, is what I give them a 100% to give them the love they deserve, instead, I became a stranger to my own needs. I stopped pursuing my own passions and silenced my own voice, all to keep the peace and ensure they felt whole. It took me a long time to see that I couldn't truly love someone if I had completely abandoned myself. Loving someone shouldn't have meant losing my identity—it should have meant finding someone who respected the person I already am.

When Love Cost Me Peace

I finally realized that true love shouldn't feel like a constant battle to stay afloat. For a long time, I thought love was meant to be intense and draining, but I’ve learned that the best kind of love is actually quiet. It’s the kind that brings me peace of mind rather than constant anxiety. I stopped looking for the "spark" that keeps me on edge and started looking for the "calm" that lets me sleep at night. Now understand that the cost of a relationship is just too great if it compromises my sense of self or mental well-being. Real love is a place where I finally feel whole and at peace; it doesn't require me to lose myself.
