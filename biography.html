My Own Life

Written by Luis Suing

Childhood Years

1I grew up in Quezon City, a place filled with busy streets and warm memories. 2I remember having a play friend who came over almost every afternoon. 3We always met from 3 p.m. to 6 p.m., which became the happiest hours of my childhood. 4We played outside endlessly as if time did not exist. 5Those moments made me feel free and excited every day. 6Even though I enjoyed playing, I was the kind of kid who cried easily. 7Whenever someone bullied me, tears would come before I could stop them. 8I always tried to fight back, but I still ended up crying in the end. 9Despite that, those experiences helped me understand my emotions early on. 10My childhood also came with family issues that shaped me in difficult ways. 11Those challenges forced me to grow up faster than I expected. 12They taught me that life is not always simple or kind. 13Still, I found comfort in small joys around me. 14I loved the afternoons that felt peaceful and full of laughter. 15I learned to appreciate the people who stayed by my side. 16My neighborhood became a part of my identity. 17The streets I played on became symbols of both happiness and struggle. 18My childhood shaped the sensitivity I carry today. 19It also built the strength I continue to develop. 20Looking back, those early years form a story I will always carry with me.

Teenage Years

1My teenage years began when I entered Quezon City Academy in Grade 7. 2I stayed there until Grade 12, collecting countless memories along the way. 3School life was often hectic and filled with assignments, deadlines, and responsibilities. 4Even so, a part of me still feels nostalgic when I remember those times. 5I formed friendships that made everything feel lighter and more meaningful. 6During my free time, I enjoyed watching shows and playing video games to relax. 7I also had achievements, although they changed from year to year. 8I joined contests occasionally, even when I wasn’t confident. 9Trying still made me proud because I knew it helped me grow. 10My teenage years taught me patience and discipline. 11Some days felt overwhelming, while others were filled with laughter that erased stress. 12I learned the importance of balancing fun and responsibility. 13I also discovered more about myself—what motivated me and what slowed me down. 14Not everything was easy, but effort always mattered. 15Those years were far from perfect, yet they strengthened me. 16They shaped my identity and prepared me for the next chapter of my life.

College Years

1My college experience began when I entered the University of the East – Manila. 2I am currently a second-year BSIT student, still learning and adapting every day. 3At first, college felt overwhelming because everything was new. 4There was more work, more pressure, and more expectations. 5Over time, I slowly learned how to manage my schedule better. 6I met friends who made the journey easier and more enjoyable. 7They supported me during stressful moments and shared lessons that helped me grow. 8I joined several organizations within the university, each shaping my leadership and social skills. 9I serve as a business chairperson in one organization and stay active in three others. 10Through these experiences, I learned independence, teamwork, and responsibility. 11There are days when college is exhausting, but there are also days filled with opportunities and fun. 12Every challenge teaches me something valuable. 13I learned to ask for help when I needed it. 14I realized that growth comes from both struggles and successes. 15College helped me set new goals for myself and for my future. 16It continues to shape who I am, day by day, as I work toward becoming the person I want to be.
