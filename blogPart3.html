Astronomy to IT:
A Vision for My Career
Authored by: Luis Michael L. Suing | January 28, 2026

Finding My Direction Through the Stars

I became interested in astronomy because I was curious about space and how the universe works. I often looked at the night sky and asked questions about stars and planets. Over time, this interest became something more serious. I realized that learning about astronomy made me motivated to study and improve myself. It helped me understand that there is a wide world beyond what we see every day. This interest made me think about my future and the kind of career I want to pursue.

Choosing a Path That Truly Matters to Me

As I reached the point where I had to choose a college course, my interest in astronomy was still guiding my thoughts about the future. I knew I wanted a path that would not only challenge me but also help me grow, so I chose Information Technology. I was already comfortable working with computers and enjoyed solving problems, which made IT feel like a natural choice. As I continued learning, I realized that the skills I gain in IT—such as programming, logical thinking, and understanding technology—can support my dream of studying astronomy. Instead of leaving my passion behind, I found a way to move closer to it through a practical and meaningful path.

  • Continued interest in astronomy as a guiding passion
  • Choosing Information Technology as a practical college course
  • Confidence in working with computers and technology
  • Building skills in programming, hardware, and logical thinking
  • Using IT knowledge to support future goals in astronomy and data analysis

When I started choosing a course for college, my interest in astronomy was still clear to me. I decided to take Information Technology not only because it is in demand, but because it is something I am capable of learning and understanding. I am familiar with computers and I enjoy working with technology and solving problems. Studying IT helps me develop skills such as programming, hardware peripherals, and logical thinking. These skills can support my long-term goal of studying astronomy, especially in areas that rely on technology and data analysis. By choosing IT, I am taking a practical step while still working toward my passion for astronomy.

My Future Career

  • Importance of self-understanding in career decisions
  • Balancing passion (astronomy) with practical skills (IT)
  • Learning that career choices require effort and commitment
  • Aligning personal interests with abilities
  • Plans to continuously improve IT skills while pursuing astronomy

Looking back at my choices, I realized that understanding myself is important when deciding on a career. My interest in astronomy continues to guide my long-term goal, while taking Information Technology helps me build practical skills that I can use along the way. I learned that choosing a career is not only about what is in demand, but also about what I am capable of and willing to work hard for. My takeaway is that growth comes from aligning passion with ability. Moving forward, I plan to continue improving my IT skills while keeping my focus on astronomy, knowing that both can support my future. This path gives me confidence and direction as I work toward my career goals.
